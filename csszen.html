The Rold to Enlightenment

Littering a dark and dreary road lay the past relics of brower-speific tags, incompatible DOMs,broken CSSsupport, and abandoned browers.

We must clear the mind of the past. Web enlighterment has been achieved thanks to the tireless efforts of like the W3C, WaSP,and the major browser creators.

The CSS Zen Garden invites you to relax and meditate on the important lessons of the masters. Begin to see with clarity. Learn to use the time-honored techniques in new and invigorating fashion. Become one with the web.

requirements

Where possible, we would like to see mostly CSS 1 & 2 usage. CSS 3 & 4 should be limited to widely-supported elements only, or strong fallbacks should be provided. The CSS Zen Garden is about functional, practical CSSand not latest bleeding-edge tricks viewable by 2% of the browsing public. The only real requirement we have is that your CSSvalidates.

Luckily, designing this way shows how well various browers have implemented should see fairly consistent result accross most modern browsers. Due to the sheer number of user agents on the web these days — especially when you factor in mobile — pixel-perfect layouts mays not be possible across every platform. That’s okay, but do test in as many as you can. Your design should work in at least IE9+ and the latest Chrome, Firefox, iOS and Android browers (run by over 90% of the population).

We ask that you submit original artwork. Please respect copyright laws. Please keep objectionable material to a minium, and try to incorporate unique and interesting visual themes to your work. We’re well past the point needing another garden-related design.

This is a leaning exercise as well as a demonstration. You retain full copyright on your graphics (with limited exceptions, see submission guidelines,but we ask you release your CSS under a Creative Commons Liscense identical to the one on this site so that others may learn from your work.
